Early Life

Christopher Columbus' early life started in August - October, 1451 when he was born in Genoa, Italy to parents, Dominico Colombo, and Susanna. He also had a sister named Bianchenetta. His dad was a wool merchant and sparked the idea of sailing into young Christopher's mind. As his life progressed, he served an apprenticeship with his father's merchant business, and throughout this his interest in sailing continued to grow, and when he reached his teenage years, Christopher Columbus managed to strike a job on a Merchant Ship, and start his sailing career. He continued sailing until 1470, he held this position until he was attacked by the French off the coast of Portugal. After this attack, he headed back to begin his career as an explorer.

Second and Third Voyages

Return to the Bahamas
The entire trip back to the Bahamas was once again utilizing the same route they used for the first voyage. Except this time, there was 17 ships, instead of 3. The funding was much larger(King Ferdinand of Spain) this time around, following the news of the discoveries made in the first expedition. Knowing that Christopher Columbus had some potential, patrons were much more willing to invest time, and money into his expeditions. In this second expedition, Christopher Columbus established many more outposts, and areas, as well as fortified each area. This expedition, and voyage proved to be much more impactful then the first. In 1496, Columbus set off back to Spain, leaving his brothers in-charge of the settlements established in the Caribbean Islands. Upon arrival, he expressed his ideas for his third voyage, at the time, Spain was at war with France. Once again, he managed to gain a third investment from Ferdinand, and set sail with 6 other ships. This time, he set further west, and gained some of the more central-american colonies under his settlement. Eventually, Columbus set sail back to Spain, making this the last of his Caribbean Voyages.
